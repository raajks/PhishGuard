Abstract

Phishing attacks have become a major cybersecurity threat, leveraging social engineering techniques to deceive users into disclosing sensitive information. With the rapid growth of online services, phishing strategies are continuously evolving, making their detection increasingly challenging.

This paper presents a comprehensive survey of phishing detection techniques, covering the progression from traditional approaches to advanced machine learning and deep learning-based methods. Existing techniques are broadly categorized into four groups: list-based methods, heuristic and similarity-based methods, machine learning-based approaches, and deep learning-based models. For each category, the paper discusses fundamental concepts, commonly used features and datasets, as well as their advantages and limitations.

Furthermore, a comparative analysis of these techniques is provided, highlighting key challenges such as zero-day attack detection, data imbalance, feature selection, and model generalization. The study also identifies future research directions aimed at enhancing the accuracy, scalability, and adaptability of phishing detection systems.

This survey aims to serve as a structured and up-to-date reference for researchers and practitioners in the field of cybersecurity.
